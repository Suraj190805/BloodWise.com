Overview

Welcome to BloodWise, your one-stop destination for information on blood donation, management, and awareness. Our mission is to bridge the gap between donors and recipients while ensuring that blood stocks are maintained and easily accessible during emergencies.

Blood donation is a vital, life-saving act. It not only provides a critical resource for those in need but also strengthens community bonds. By donating blood, you help save lives, support medical treatments, and contribute to a healthier society.

Explore our site to learn more about how you can contribute, find compatible donors or recipients, and educate yourself on safe practices and precautions related to blood donation.

About Blood & Blood Types

🔴 Universal Donor

O-negative is known as the universal donor, as it can be given to patients of all blood types.

🔵 Universal Recipient

AB-positive is known as the universal recipient, as individuals with this blood type can receive from any donor.

⚠️ Diseases Transmitted Through Blood

  • Hepatitis B and C
  • HIV/AIDS
  • Malaria
  • Syphilis

✅ Precautions to Avoid Blood-borne Diseases

  • Always use sterile needles and syringes.
  • Ensure proper screening of donated blood.
  • Practice safe blood transfusion protocols.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Who can donate blood? ▼
Generally, healthy individuals aged 18-65 and weighing at least 50kg can donate blood.
2. How often can I donate blood? ▼
You can donate blood every 12 weeks (for men) or 16 weeks (for women).
3. Is blood donation safe? ▼
Yes, it is safe. Sterile equipment is used, and there is no risk of infection.
4. How long does blood donation take? ▼
The process takes about 45 minutes, including registration, donation, and post-donation rest.
